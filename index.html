About ISERC I/O

ISERC I/O is a monthly seminar hosted by students of ISERC with a main goal of providing a platform for students to present and exchange their summer research experiences and to participate in an engaging and probative discussion of the ideas presented. In this event, students of ISERC are fully commited to ISERC I/O as a mean to share knowledge and bring new approaches to advancing enterpreneurship in science and research.

Schedule

  • Evaluation of Pharmacological Effects of Chemotherapeutics on Physiological Properties of Triple Negative Breast Cancer Cell Line.

    Speaker: Priya Murmu

    Click to View Abstract05:40 - 05:55 PM
    •
    Abstract:
    Cancer can be defined as an abnormal growth of cellls to form tumor, which tend to proliferate in an uncontrolled manner and metastasize distant locations in the body. Breast cancer is the second most common cause of mortality in women. Triple Negative Breast Cancers(TNBCs) are the most aggressive; they reoccur early and show poorer prognosis when compared to other tumor types. Hormone therapies and anti-HER2 therapies fail to contain the tumor growth for which patients with TNBCs are suggested to undergo chemotherapy. Chemotherapy is initiated either with an anthracycline (e.g. doxorubicin or epirubicin) or a taxane (e.g. docetaxel or paclitaxel). In the present study, in vitro experiments were designed to evaluate the pharmacological concentration of these drugs against the proliferation and migration of an aggressive TNBC cell MDA-MB-468.
  • Population Dynamics of Plant-Herbivore-Carnivore Ecosystem with Mobility Strategies

    Speaker: Archishman Saha

    Click to View Abstract06:00 - 06:25 PM
    •
    Abstract:
    The Plant-Herbivore-Carnivore ecosystem is modeled by the Hastings-Powell model. We assign two distinct strategies to the herbivores and carnivores - sessility and mobility, and assume that carnivores seek to maximize their per capita predation rate, while herbivores seek to maximize their per capita food intake rate while trying not to get caught. A separate analysis of population dynamics and strategy evolution is carried out, with the aim of investigating the evolution of mobility strategies in herbivores and carnivores.

    • Fundamental Rights

      Speaker: Himanshu Shekhar

      View Abstract05:15 - 05:45 PM
      •
      Abstract:
      The fundamental rights were included in the constitution because they were considered essential for the development of the personality of every individual and to preserve human dignity. The writers of the constitution regarded democracy to no avail if civil liberties, like freedom of speech and religion were not recognised and protected by the State. According to them, "democracy" is, in essence, a government by the opinion and therefore, the means of formulating public opinion should be secured to the people of a democratic nation. For this purpose, the constitution guaranteed to all the citizens of India the freedom of speech and expression and various other freedoms in the form of the fundamental rights.
